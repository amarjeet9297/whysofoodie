Why so Foodie

A foodie is a person who has an ardent or refined interest in food and who eats food not out of hunger but due to their interest or hobby.

1. WHAAT’S CHAAT?

In the bustling streets of India, you’ll find chaat, hundreds of mouth-watering fresh snacks that have fueled billions of people for generations. Served in roadside carts helmed by masters of bold flavors and textures, chaat is alive with crunch and spice and tang. Tomatoes,onions,chillies,imli chutney and small sized lemon squeezed with topings of masaledaar chana, kairi and sev.

Dahi Puri, Bhel puri, Paani puri can be thus considered as cousins of this tangy treat.Well whether it’s the crispy combo or the mouthwatering blend of sweet & sour, we certainly are in love with this colorful fusion of tastes.

You’ve never tasted Indian food like this before. #LetsChaat

2. Garma Garam Aalu?

Why aalu is considered to be the best all time. Heres to the fans who love aalu kanda bhajjis, garama garam vade and moong dal bhajjis aur samose. Barish ka mausam and garma garam bhajji thele ke ho ya mom k hath ke, all are acceptable with one cup of tea. The smell of bhajjis when are fried in oil is so lip smacking that no one can ignore having more after finishing one.
Though Mirchi Bajjis vary in different regions but the Hydrabadi styled are certainly the best all over India.

Bole toh Vada Pao, a spicy delight from ‘Aamchi Mumbai’ Maharashtra runs on this Indian version of burger. It is every Mumbaikar’s take – and- go quick bite. Everyone from student to elite businessmen flock onto these vadapav stalls almost every day.

It is believed that there is no better place than a roadside stall to enjoy this authentic Marathi cuisine.

3. Manchurian ya Noodle?

A tint of China in India, with modifications made to cater to local palates. Chinese food staples such as rice, noodles, soya sauce, shezwan chutney with some added pepper and flakes be every tongues favourite.
Tangy spicy red sauce and steaming hot dumplings loaded with veggies or chicken is something that we just cannot resist while walking past the stall on road. Spicy hot sizzling variety one gets, from children t o adults, cheap and mouth watering has laid streeds to top chinese flavour in everyones mouth.

Soups be favourite of mine when i fall sick with tonsils. Best remedy though to have warm soups for curing sore throat.

4. South Chalein?

Coconut ki chutney with medu vada is all time favourite for people who go to office. Sambar rice in lunch and idli ya dosa for dinner…sort hai zindagi. A big heaping plate of rice with a little bowl on the side of sambaar with plopping my papads in like a cracker. South ki baat hi alag hai.

South gae aur filter coffee nai pi??

5. Meethe mei?

Dessert is probably the most important stage of the meal, since it will be the last thing your guests remember before they pass out all over the table.Gulab jaumun se leke jalebi rabri khao toh jano
Bano toh dessertian vegetarian toh har koi hota hai

6. Khai k?

Ram Pyaari, Meenakshi, Lal Pari, Panchrangi, Kasturi, Surabhi, Madhuri, Heena, Dil Kush, Mohini, Calcutta………. et al – all names that are more colourful than the others; and very much in the veins of the die-hard connoisseurs of paan. For in these, lie the delectable, tempting, addictive and immensely popular flavours and varieties of paan. In fact, the habit of eating paan has been so popular that it has slowly crept in to become a part of our heritage and culture.

O Bhaiya, ek paan dena

7. Desh mei Visdeshi cheeezzzzzz?

Cheese atop crackers, a dreamy pizza with melted cheese, a piping hot bowl of mac n cheese, a heavenly Chicken Cheese pizza, and the list goes on. Cheese is the one ingredient which is used worldwide and is loved by over half the world’s population. A turophile is a person who loves cheese. 

Are you turophile? If not be one.

Does an image of a piping cheesy sub flash across your eyes ? Check them now.

8. lazeez-e-kebab

Cubes of chicken mutton cut and skewed.
 Kebab dishes can consist of cut up or ground meat or seafood, sometimes with fruits and vegetables; cooked on a skewer over a fire, or like a hamburger on a grill,barbeque nation pe khae ho??? Keema ka asli mazza pav k sath aata hai.

9. Shera di Shaan

Punjabiyon ki lassi. Eat street kulche chole with utterly butterly topping of white makhan or butter on it. Pet bhar jae but swaad na jae the chole are soaked and cooked in spices and slow cooked with onions, chillies and ginger garlic paste. Garam Masala pad jae toh chole khil jae.
